About SEER!

The Surveillance, Epidemiology, and End Results (SEER) program of the National Cancer Institute (NCI) is a source of epidemiologic information on the incidence and survival rates of cancer in the United States. SEER collects and publishes cancer incidence and survival data from population-based cancer registries covering approximately 28% of the population of the United States. SEER coverage includes 26% of African Americans, 41% of Hispanics, 43% of American Indians and Alaska Natives, 54% of Asians, and 71% of Hawaiian/Pacific Islanders.
